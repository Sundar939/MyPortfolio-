Projects

Improving the Strength of Friction Stir Welded Joint by Double-Sided Welding for AA6065 (11/2021 - 03/2022)

  • Undertook a project to enhance the strength of friction stir welded joints in AA6065, a widely used aluminum alloy in aerospace and automotive industries.
  • Selected optimal welding parameters such as tool rotation speed, traverse speed, and axial force based on the material and welding setup.
  • Ensured thorough surface preparation of the material by cleaning and removing impurities or oxide.
  • Executed simultaneous welding on both joint sides to diminish heat input and provide uniform welding.
  • Employed adequate clamping and fixturing to prevent material movement or distortion during welding.
  • Performed post-weld treatments like heat treatment, hot isostatic pressing, or surface finishing to facilitate the strength and integrity of the joint.
  • Successfully bolstered the strength of the FSW joint for AA6065 through double-sided welding.

Project: Improving the Strength of Friction Stir Welded Joint by Double-Sided Welding for AA6065

  • Welding Parameters: Shared insights on selecting optimal parameters such as tool rotation speed, traverse speed, and axial force. Discussed the impact of these parameters on the quality and strength of the weld.

  • Post-Weld Treatments: Discussed various post-weld treatments, including heat treatment, hot isostatic pressing, and surface finishing. Explained how these treatments enhance the strength and integrity of the weld.
  • Project Outcomes: Presented the successful results of the project, emphasizing the improved strength of the friction stir welded joints achieved through the double-sided welding method.
